Luzon


Location:Vigan
Description:capital of Ilocos Sur, Philippines. Known as the best-preserved, planned Spanish colonial town in Asia, it is famous for its cobblestone Calle Crisologo, ancestral houses fusing European and Asian architecture, and local delicacies like bagnet and empanada.
Location:Intramuros Manila
Description:is Manila's oldest district and the historic heart of the Philippine capital.
Location:Mayon Volcano
Description: an active stratovolcano in Albay, Philippines, famed globally for its near-perfect, symmetrical conical shape.
Location:Banaue Rice Terraces
Description: These are ancient rice terraces carved into the mountains by local tribes. Think of them like giant, green steps climbing up into the clouds.
Location:Baguio City
Description: Known as the "Summer Capital," this mountain city features cool weather, pine trees, and fresh strawberries.


Visayas


Location:White Beach, Boracay
Description:Beach with powdery white sand. The water is clear and great for swimming.
Location:Chocolate Hills, Bohol
Description: a unique geological formation of over 1,200 symmetrical, cone-shaped hills spread across 50 square kilometers.
Location:Kawasan Falls
Description:This waterfall has bright, freezing blue water. It is very popular for canyoneering.
Location:San Juanico Bridge
Description:This bridge stretches 2 kilometers long. It connects two islands: Samar and Leyte. It is a beautiful engineering marvel.
Location:Kalanggaman Island
Description:This island is world-famous for its strikingly long, bone-white sandbars that stretch out into crystal-clear turquoise waters. It is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and taking postcard-worthy photos.


Mindanao


Location:Siargao Island
Description:Known globally as the Surfing Capital of the Philippines, Siargao is famous for its iconic "Cloud 9" wave. Beyond surfing, it features coconut tree-lined roads, stunning tidal pools, and pristine island-hopping spots.
Location:Hinatuan Enchanted River
Description:This is a breathtaking, deep sapphire-blue saltwater river hidden inside a lush jungle. It is considered "enchanted" because its clear water appears to have no visible source, flowing directly from an underwater cave system into the Pacific Ocean.
Location:Camiguin Island
Description:Often called the "Island Born of Fire," Camiguin is a pear-shaped volcanic island packed with natural wonders. It is home to seven volcanoes, cold and hot springs, waterfalls, and the famous Sunken Cemetery—a historical graveyard that sank underwater during a volcanic eruption.
Location:Mount Apo
Description:Standing majestically at 2,956 meters, Mount Apo is the highest peak in the Philippines. It serves as a holy mountain for local indigenous tribes and a bucket-list climb for trekkers.
Location:Lake Sebu
Description:This peaceful highland lake is a major cultural and ecotourism destination. It is the ancestral home of the T'boli tribe, who are renowned for their intricate T'nalak weaving.
